Every sidebar entry, every canvas

A full Navidrome client on iPad — with a real sidebar and a real detail pane.

Every browse mode Navidrome exposes, surfaced in a persistent sidebar. Album grids that actually use the extra pixels. Color-gradient Genre mood cards. A Finder-style Directories browser. Internet radio with the stream URLs visible. And when you want lyrics or the queue, they dock alongside the main view instead of hiding it.

01 / Home

Sidebar on the left, featured album up top, shelves underneath.

Persistent sidebar with Home, Search, Now Playing, and the full Library entries. The main pane opens on Pick Up Where You Left Off with a large cover-art card and a Play button, followed by a Quick Play row — Shuffle Mix, Random Album, Favorites, Random Artist — and shelves for Most Played and Recently Added. The mini player stays docked at the bottom, always in reach.

02 / Albums

Seven columns of cover art. Every album, one glance.

The Albums view uses the extra iPad pixels to show 7 albums across in a fluid grid — no scaled-up phone view, no oversize tiles. Artist and year metadata under each cover, tap for the detail page. Every album on your Navidrome server shown the way the platform was meant to be browsed: visually.

03 / Genres

A wall of color-coded mood cards.

Genre browsing reimagined: each genre gets its own color-gradient mood card with the genre name and a live song count from your server. Tap any card for a filtered album view. Everything your Navidrome server has tagged shows up — Rock, Metal, Hard Rock, Alternative, Dance, Electronic, Reggae, Hip-Hop, and every niche your library has accumulated.

04 / Lyrics

Time-synced lyrics docked alongside Now Playing.

When your Navidrome server has LRC lyrics attached, iPad mounts them in the right half of the screen while cover art and transport stay visible on the left. Current line highlighted in accent orange, past lines fade out, upcoming lines wait. LRC, SRT, embedded, and Subsonic getLyricsBySongId responses all supported. Falls back gracefully to plain text.

05 / Up Next

The queue lives in the right pane, not a modal sheet.

Tap the queue icon on iPad and Up Next opens as a dedicated right-side panel — cover art and transport stay visible on the left. Scroll the queue without losing your place in the music. Drag to reorder, swipe to remove, Clear All to wipe the queue without stopping playback. The server syncs the new order in the background.

06 / Internet Radio

Every station, with its stream URL visible.

The Radios tab lists every internet radio station stored in your Navidrome server as a full-width table — station name up top, stream URL exposed underneath. Useful for debugging, honest about where the audio comes from. MP3, AAC, OGG, and anything else your server streams. One tap starts playback in the bottom mini player.

07 / Directories

The only Subsonic iPad client with a real folder browser.

NaviBeat exposes the Directories API most other clients hide. Browse your music library the way it's actually stored on disk — artist folders, year folders, compilation folders, whatever structure you've chosen. Finder-style breadcrumbs at the top, chevron rows beneath, Back button in the navigation bar. Works with Unicode, non-Latin scripts, and symlinks alike.

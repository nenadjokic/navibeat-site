Every tab, every screen

A full Navidrome client on Apple TV — not a scaled-up phone app.

Every Navidrome feature you use on the web is here: Albums, Artists, Genres, Playlists, Favorites, Internet Radio, Search with history, artist biographies from Last.fm, Hi-Res badges, and time-synced lyrics. Built from the ground up for the Siri Remote and the 10-foot interface.

01 / Home

Featured Album, Quick Play, Recently Added.

The Home tab opens on a rotating Featured Album hero, followed by a Quick Play row (Favourites, Shuffle Mix, Random Album, Random Artist) and a Recently Added shelf. One press on the Siri Remote and music is playing — no menu digging.

02 / Library

Albums, Artists, Random, Genres, Favorites.

The Library tab mirrors Navidrome’s browse modes exactly. Albums grid with year and genre metadata. Artists as circular portraits with album counts. Random shuffle across your whole collection. Genre filtering. Your Favorites tab, synced from the server.

03 / Artist Detail

Biographies from Last.fm, album grid below.

Every artist page opens with a full biography above a chronological album grid. Press Play to shuffle the entire discography. Complete artist and album metadata, on a self-hosted stack you own.

04 / Time-Synced Lyrics

Every word, exactly when it lands.

Drop an .lrc file next to your track, or let NaviBeat pull synced lyrics from your server. Read them on the big screen. The current line highlights and scrolls automatically — no interaction needed.

05 / Now Playing

Transport, queue, and info — all in one view.

The Now Playing screen shows full-screen album art, transport controls tuned for the Siri Remote, an Up Next queue you can reorder, and a swipe-in lyrics panel. No modal stacking, no buried menus.

06 / Internet Radio

Your Navidrome radio stations, on the big screen.

Internet radio stations configured in Navidrome appear automatically. Tap any station, the stream starts immediately. Skip to the next station without returning to the list.
